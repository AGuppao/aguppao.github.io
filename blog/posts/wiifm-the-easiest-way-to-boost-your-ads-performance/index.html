WIIFM: The Easiest Way to Boost Your Ad's Performance

Abhay Gupta

24/11/2024

Summary

"What's in it for me?". Anyone who sees your ad will ask this. Make sure to answer it.

When was the last time you had a conversation with someone who only talked about themselves the entire time?

Did they also conveniently forget to give you a chance to say anything?

Well... this idea of someone only talking about themselves is quite common in marketing.

And that's not a good thing.

It's a quick way to kill any advertising efforts.

So, why is only talking about yourself such a sin in advertising?


Many Companies Only Talk About Themselves

Just to clarify, when I say "companies talking about themselves", I mean whenever a company is talking about its achievements, awards, stories, history etc.

And this can be ok sometimes. I mean, if someone asks.

But, when it comes to advertising, then only talking about yourself is a problem.

Why?

Well... just like that person who only talked about themselves, you're forgetting to include the prospect in the "conversation".

You're not giving them a chance to tell you what they want.

And this is where most companies fail - they don't ask their prospects what they want.

So, why should you avoid only talking about yourself in your ads?


Only Talking About Yourself is a Waste of Money

There are so many reasons to avoid only talking about yourself in your ads. And I won't go over all of them.

But here are some of the main ones:

  • No one cares: Prospects don't care about you or your company. They care about their problems and how quickly you can solve them.

  • Lack of focus: Your ad's not focused on a specific action you want people to take (like contacting you).

  • Boring: Your ad will have a lot of information that no one will read. These text overloads are boring.

So why is this bad?

Well... it wastes money!

After all, you've paid for the ad.

So, what should you include in your ad?


WIIFM

Now, I've already gone over how to make a good ad. And I won't go over all of it again.

But there is one thing that I haven't properly explained yet: WIIFM.

"What's 'WIIFM'?"

WIIFM is a super simple question.

If all of your marketing is designed to answer this question, then there's basically no reason for you to fail.

WIIFM stands for: "What's in it for Me?"

So, how can you use this concept?


Why WIIFM is the Most Important Question Ever

Put yourself in the prospect's shoes, look at your ad and ask the question "What's in it for me?".

If you can answer the question, then you're on the right track.

So, what makes this question super effective?

If you purely focus on answering this question, then you can cut out all of the extra stuff you don't need from your ad.

You won't even be tempted to put in all of the extra stuff to begin with, since it doesn't answer the question.


The Only Extra Things You Can Include

Now, there are some cases where you can talk about yourself in your ad AND still answer WIIFM.

For example, you can include what other services your company provides.

You could include important certifications you have.

That's about it. Anything more personal (like your company's history and achievements) is just waffle.

And if you are going to include this stuff, I have two main points to make:

  1. Keep it short: Get straight to the point. Avoid going into too much detail.

  2. Keep it small: Make sure it doesn't take up a lot of space on the ad. You don't want to distract people from the main message of your ad.


So, let's stop putting in all of the extra stuff you don't need in your ad. Simply focus on the main message - your offer.

Don't be that person that only talks about themselves.

Take a look at your ad and ask "What's in it for me?". And if you can answer the question, then you have a good ad.
