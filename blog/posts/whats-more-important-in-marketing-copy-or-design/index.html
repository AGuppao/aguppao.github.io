What's More Important in Marketing: Copy or Design?

Abhay Gupta

04/02/25

Summary

Poor design can damage your marketing campaign, but poor copy will completely kill it. In general, good copy is more important than good design, but bad copy is far worse than poor design.

Have you ever seen an ad and thought: "What's the point of this ad?"

Or, maybe it was a website and you wondered: "What's this website even for?"

And it took you a while to decipher what it was about.

Well, you're not alone.

This has happened to me too. So many times in fact.

And it all boils down to two things:

  1. Poor Copy

  2. Poor Design

And this is where a lot of people get stuck.

So, what's more important in marketing, copy or design?


What is "Copy" and "Design"?

Firstly, let me clarify what I mean by "copy" and "design".

These are both generic terms that describe pretty much every form of marketing.

Be it a Meta Ad or your own website.

"Copy" refers to the text-related content of your marketing. I like to think of it as the "script" (this is more accurate for videos).

"Design" refers to the appearance of your marketing. This includes stuff like the color scheme, fonts, images etc.

So, what is it about copy and design that causes many people to get stuck?


Common Mistakes with Copy and Design

Let's go back to the beginning of this blog.

Do you remember that ad that just didn't make any sense?

Well, it's because either (or both) copy and design were wrong.

And it's sometimes super obvious too.

Examples of Poor Copy

For example, poor copy could look like this:

  • There's no offer, leaving the viewer thinking: "what's the point of this ad?".

  • The first thing you see is the company's name.

  • Text overloads about the company's history / mission.

  • No call to action.

Generally, there aren't that many things that can go wrong with copy.

But, making these mistakes will kill any marketing campaign.

Examples of Poor Design

Now, I've seen far worse examples of poor design, such as:

  • The logo takes up 90% of the ad space.

  • Random, unrelated, AI-generated or stock images.

  • Colour schemes that would give you an epileptic fit.

  • Tiny fonts that blend into the background, so they're impossible to read.

  • ...

Seriously, I could go on and on about poor design.

Well, because there are so many tiny things that can go wrong.

But I'll talk about that in a different blog.

Now, poor design won't completely kill your marketing campaign, but it will turn away a good amount of potential customers.

And this leads to the main question...


Copy vs Design: Which is More Important?

I guess the question really is: "What turns away more potential customers, poor copy or poor design?".

And the answer is simple: Copy is more important than design!

Why?

Well, imagine you see a well-designed leaflet for a company.

The font sizes are fine, the colour scheme doesn't burn your eyes and the images are nice.

But, there's no text.

Could you tell me what the point of the leaflet is?

Probably not, right?

And this is where copy comes in.

Copy tells you exactly what issues the company is there to solve and how you can contact them.

Without these two things in your marketing, no one would ever know what your offer is.

Worse yet, even if they did know the offer, they wouldn't be able to contact you because you didn't give them any information.


The General Rule

In short, good copy is better than good design.

You can still do well in marketing if your design is bad, just as long as your copy is good.

But if you're copy is bad... Not ideal.

Now, there are so many other subtle details about copy and design that I won't cover in this blog.

But, in upcoming blogs, I'll give a little more insight into how to write effective copy and design a captivating ad.

For now, know that good copy is far more important than good design.

Poor design can damage your marketing campaign, but poor copy will completely kill it.
