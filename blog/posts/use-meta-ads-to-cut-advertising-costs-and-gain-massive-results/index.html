Use Meta Ads to Cut Advertising Costs and Gain Massive Results

Abhay Gupta

Nov 03, 2024

Wouldn't it be awesome if you could advertise your company on TV and have the entire world see it? Imagine the publicity you'd get from that.

Well, you could do that... if you have a small country's GDP as your marketing budget.

And this is the same for almost all other forms of advertising.

Looking for a cheaper way to advertise your company?

Then, you have to use Meta Ads.

So, what makes Meta Ads the cheapest form of advertising?

And what mistakes do other companies make when using Meta Ads that you can avoid?


Why Meta Ads are the Cheapest Form of Advertising

Well... there are 2 main things:

  1. Laser focus on a specific audience

  2. Custom budgets

I've already talked about 1 before, so let's talk about 2.

In fact, the idea of a custom budget causes a lot of businesses to make a massive mistake.


What Most Businesses Get Wrong About Meta Ad Budgets

A lot of business owners think: "£5.50 one time for a coffee is a bargain. But £10/day on an ad that'll make me even more money... that's sooo overpriced."

And they'll set the ad's budget to £1/day.

In general, if you're spending more on your coffee than on ads, then you're doing it wrong.

So, why is this so bad?

Well... that ad can't reach enough people to make a difference.

Think of it like trying to promote a product through word of mouth, but only talking to one person per day.

That's too slow. You'll never reach anyone before your competition.

There's another problem with setting a tiny budget.


Meta Likes Big Spenders

Just like you, Meta wants to make as much money as possible.

They prioritise people who spend more money on ads.

Which makes sense, right?

And don't worry, you don't need to waste a fortune on ads.

You just need to find the right amount to spend.

So, no more £1/day budgets, you're better off spending between £10-£50/day (depending on your targets, of course).

And there's something even better about budgets...


Meta Only Charges Per View

You don't just set a budget of £50/day and kiss that money goodbye.

You're only charged when someone actually sees your ad. So, you're not paying upfront.

Meaning, you'll only pay for the ad when it's actually doing something useful.

Now, this is a bit oversimplified, but the point is the same.

In reality, you can tell Meta how to charge you. Per click, per impression or whatever.

The different options have different prices, of course.

"Does that mean Meta could charge me more than my daily budget?"


Meta Uses a Fixed Weekly Budget

Say you set the daily budget to £10/day.

On some days, you'll end up spending a little bit more than £10/day.

On other days, you'll spend less than £10/day.

Meta will make sure that your weekly spend doesn't exceed 7 times the daily budget.

So, with a £10/day budget, Meta won't charge you more than £70/week.

Personally, I like to set a weekly budget, divide it by 7 and tell Meta to use that amount.


So, let's start using Meta Ads to cut advertising costs and gain massive results.

No more £1/day budgets, you're better off spending between £10-£50/day on your Meta Ads.

And you're only charged when someone sees your ad, so you're not paying upfront.

Finally, set a weekly budget, divide it by 7 and tell Meta to use that amount as your daily budget.
