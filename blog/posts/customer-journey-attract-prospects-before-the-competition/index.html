Customer Journey: Attract Prospects Before the Competition

Abhay Gupta

Sep 21, 2024

You've probably faced many problems.

Maybe your car broke down. Strong winds swept away a bunch of roof tiles. Your dog was sick. Or something else.

So you did some research to find out what solutions exist. You filtered through the majority of the solutions and you picked the best one.

Congratulations, you've just been on a Customer Journey...

Sounds underwhelming?

I thought so.

But, this is a super useful concept that will help you land more clients.

So, why is the customer journey important?


Solve Problems ASAP

Your customers have a problem and they want to solve it as soon as possible.

But they don't just go straight to the solution.

There's usually a period between the prospect having a problem and them actually solving it.

So you can use the customer journey idea to reach prospects during this period.

But what actually is the customer journey?


5 Stages of a Customer Journey

The customer journey can be described in stages:

  1. Awareness - prospect becomes aware of a problem
  2. Consideration - prospect researches potential solutions
  3. Conversion - prospect chooses one of the solutions (prospect becomes a client)
  4. Retention - client develops opinions about that solution
  5. Advocacy - client shares experiences about that solution

This gives us a formula to reach prospects as fast as possible.

You can reach out to prospects, "intercept" them, before the competition does.

How?


Magically Appear in front of Prospects

Many businesses only focus on stage 3 (conversion) by directly advertising their solutions.

These guys are entering the game late. At this stage, most customers are price shopping.

Your objective is to reach the prospect early in the customer journey.

Imagine the prospect has a problem and they search for a solution.

Then BOOM... you magically appear in front of them.


Marketing for Each Stage of the Customer Journey

All you need to do is pick a few methods for each stage. Focus your efforts on stages 1, 2 and 3. Not just 3.

What are some marketing strategies for the different stages?

For convenience, here they are in a table:

StageObjectiveExamples
AwarenessProvide informative (usually short form) contentBlogs, Social media posts (legal, safety, general statistics, misconceptions etc)
ConsiderationProvide valuable (usually long form) contentE-books, Guides (common solutions, step-by-step instructions etc)
ConversionShowcase your solutionDemos, Personalised emails, Ads
RetentionMaintain engagement to encourage repeating salesNewsletters, Loyalty programs
AdvocacyConvert clients to "brand advocates"Testimonials, Referrals, Influencer marketing

These are just some examples. In reality, there are many more things you could do for each stage.

Also, by focusing on stages 1 to 3, you don't need to funnel money into stages 4 and 5. Well, the latter stages don't occur naturally of course, but they do become easier to manage.


In the future, we'll look into special cases of this customer journey stuff, two-step lead generation and retargeting.

For now, make sure you are reaching prospects sooner during the customer journey. Use a variety of marketing strategies for the different stages.
