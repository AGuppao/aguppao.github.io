Maximising Meta Ads' Performance with Effective Copy

Abhay Gupta

09/02/25

Summary

Copy is the most important part of any ad. It's basically the text content of the ad. When writing copy, avoid boring content and be sure to include an offer. You can use the 'Hook', 'Solution', 'CTA' template to write an effective script.

Meta Ads are an incredibly powerful tool to help you reach new customers and grow your business.

However, most businesses don't actually get the most out of their Meta Ads.

There are many reasons why this is the case, but one of the main ones is that they don't focus enough on the ad copy itself.

This is a massive mistake.

Copy is the most important part of anything, and if you don't get it right, then you're throwing away your money on ads that simply don't work.

So, what are the main mistakes companies make when writing copy?

And what can you do to ensure your ad copy is effective?


Copy is the Most Important Part of Anything

I've already explained what "copy" is in a previous blog.

In short, "copy" is basically the script or text content of an ad.

This is not just limited to ads.

It can also refer to other things like emails, landing pages and so on.

In any case, the copy is the most important part.

Why?

I mean, you could post an ad that's just an image... but will anyone actually understand what it's about?

More importantly, would anyone click on it?

Probably not.

And that's where the copy comes in.

It's the part where you can actually share your message.

So, what mistakes do so many companies make when writing copy?


Common Mistakes Companies Make When Writing Ad Copy

Generic, Boring Content

The worst mistake a lot of companies make is writing generic, boring copy.

Being boring leads to your ad not retaining the audience's attention.

And this is a big problem, especially on Meta (or any other digital platform).

It's so easy for the audience to swipe away.

Your entire marketing performance purely depends on whether or not you can stop them from swiping.

You need to get their attention... and retain it.

After all, No attention = No conversions.

And boring ads typically miss out on a critical thing...

Forgetting the Offer

Think about it for a second... why do companies advertise?

I mean, it's to sell more stuff so they can grow, right?

So, when they advertise their product or service, what should that ad include?

The offer! The thing you're selling.

The offer is usually wrapped up in the CTA - the action you want the audience to take (get in touch, buy now etc).

In short, No CTA = No offer = No sales = No growth... not ideal.

And there's something just as bad as forgetting the offer...

Too Many Offers

Some ads completely flip the last point... instead of making a single offer, they'll make a whole bunch of them.

Or they'll include multiple CTA's.

They'll think: "I don't want to waste money writing an individual ad for each of my services... I'll just write one ad for all of them."

This ends up overloading the ad with information.

Not to mention that this completely messes up the idea of target audiences.

A specific audience usually only needs one of your services - not all of them at once.

I mean, Meta Ads only effective because they're so targeted.

And by doing this, you're basically throwing away money.

There are a lot more mistakes companies make when writing ad copy.

And I don't want to list them all.

These are just some of the more common ones.

So, what can you do to ensure your ad copy is effective?


How To Write Effective Copy for Meta Ads

There are two main things you can do to write effective copy...

Plan Your Meta Ads

The easiest way to write effective copy is to plan / script it. Especially for videos.

Why?

Because a script can be "measured". It can be improved.

In fact, let's look at Meta video ads as an example.

A good video ad should be between 15 and 30 seconds long.

So, you record a video using your script and it lasts a minute.

Well... you can simply remove unnecessary parts of the script or merge parts together then redo the video.

Keep doing this until you are left with the most important parts of the script.

By the time you've finished, you'll have a short, effective video ad that retains the audience's attention.

The same principle applies to all other forms of advertising.

"But, what about the stuff that was cut out?"

Well... you could always recycle it and use it somewhere else, like a Meta post.

And, if you don't know where to start with writing a script, you can do the following...

Divide Your Ad into Three Sections

You can divide the copy into three sections to make it easier to write:

  1. Hook - How will you get the audience's attention? I like to write a question that highlights the problem.

  2. Solution - what's your solution and why is it better than the other options?

  3. CTA - What do you want the audience to do (get in touch, buy now etc)?

It's so much easier to do it this way versus staring at a blank page for hours.


So, "copy" is the most important part of any ad.

There are many simple mistakes to avoid to improve your ad's performance, such as being boring and forgetting the offer.

Be sure to write a plan for your ad by dividing it into three sections: hook, problem and solution.

Finally, use this plan to cut out irrelevant parts and rewrite the ad to make it effective.
