Meta Ads vs Boosts: Why You Should Never Use Boosts

Abhay Gupta

Nov 10, 2024

Meta Ads are awesome.

But, the Ad Manager's user interface is very complicated. Especially if you're new to it.

Don't worry, Meta has a feature that can bypass all of the complexities, Boosts.

And it's awful.

So, what actually are Boosts and why should you avoid using it?


What are Boosts?

Before Boosts, Meta noticed that not that many people were using Meta Ads, so were missing out on potential revenue.

They realised that the Ads Manager was quite complicated. So, to get more people spending money on Meta, they introduced Boosts.

The Boost feature is a super simplified version of Meta Ads.

In short, you would create a regular post, then pay Meta to show that post (Boost it) to a larger audience.

Sounds great, right?

Well... not exactly.

There are many problems with Boosts.

In fact, many people are throwing away their money on Boosts when they should be using Meta Ads.

So, what's wrong with Boosts?


Boosts are a Neutered Version of Meta Ads

Not seeing any results when using Boosts?

Well, that's normal. After all, Boosts are just a neutered version of regular ads.

I mean, you are losing almost all control over the ad:

  • You're limited to specific types of posts.

  • You can't effectively target a particular audience based on an action you want to take.

  • You can't run the ad on both Facebook and Instagram.

Actually, that last point is super important.

Why?

Because you have to create a separate post for each platform and Boost each post separately - which is expensive.

And, if you're using Meta IOS, you have to pay a 30% service fee to Apple just to use Boosts - not ideal.

In fact, there are so many problems with Boosts that it's hard to explain them all. And I won't bore you with all of them.

So, what should you use instead of Boosts?


Use Meta Ads for Massive Results

Now, the UI does look complicated.

But, it won't take a huge amount of time to get used to.

And it's absolutely worth it.

You can create a single ad and have it show on Facebook and Instagram.

You have full control over the ad including an entire list of options.

You are given a huge list of metrics to help you figure out what's wrong with your ad.

And the best thing?

You're not charged upfront for using Meta Ads, unlike Boosts.

So, if your ad doesn't work as expected, you haven't wasted all of your money on it.

And I've already talked about the costs of using Meta Ads before.


So, no more using Boosts, unless you hate money.

Instead, you have to use Meta Ads.

After all, it gives you more options, better targeting and most importantly... WAY more results.
